Evan L. Whipple is a native Las Vegan (Las Vegas, Nevada) currently painting and teaching Art in Bountiful, Utah where he has a studio and gallery. His painting specializes in still life and landscape. He teaches watercolor, acrylic, and oil painting using mainly larger canvases.

Mr. Whipple was educated at Brigham Young University (Provo, UT) receiving a BS Degree---a composite of History and Sociology. He continued to finish a MA Degree in Mass Communications emphasizing Marketing, Advertising and Public Relations, later being accredited and certified in PR by the Public Relations Society of America (PRSA). His business and educational experience includes positions with major corporations and agencies in Europe and the United States. He has taught at a Community College, Secondary schools, and with his own students.

Seeking a formal education in Art, Mr. Whipple sought out two recognized art Master's, Mr. Sigfried Hahn and Mr. Howard Wexler in Albuquerque, NM studying for over two years. They are known for their research, publication (Famous Artist's Magazine), and application of the Maroguer Medium or, "wet-on-wet" pioneer painting technique.

These Master's taught Mr. Whipple the techniques of drawing, balance, layout, perspective, color mixing, hue and value coloring, as well as other techniques such as pallet knife painting. He also taught art and calligraphy at a local University.

Mr. Whipple is known for his bright colors, skies, and the skilled technique of pallet knife art painting. Some of his oil paints are over 100 years old.

Mr. Whipple currently paints and teaches watercolor, acrylic, and oil painting. He was an art instructor for the local Michael's Arts and Crafts store in Bountiful teaching weekly. He is also nationally certified by Grumbacher as a Fine Art Instructor nationally. As a member of the Bountiful Utah Art Center, Mr. Whipple contributes art pieces for public display.
